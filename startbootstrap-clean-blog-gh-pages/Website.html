What is backcountry skiing?

Backcountry skiing — also called off-piste, sidecountry, or ski touring — means skiing terrain outside of controlled ski resort boundaries. Skiers hike or skin uphill using climbing skins attached to the base of their skis, then descend untouched powder through open bowls, gladed trees, and steep couloirs.

The rewards are unmatched: solitude, jaw-dropping scenery, and first tracks in fresh snow. But the backcountry is also a serious environment. Avalanche awareness, navigation skills, and the right equipment are non-negotiable.

Best destinations

  • Wasatch Range, Utah
  • Chamonix, France
  • Hokkaido, Japan
  • Valdez, Alaska
  • La Grave, France
  • Revelstoke, BC

Key skills to develop

  • Avalanche rescue & airbag use
  • Beacon/probe/shovel proficiency
  • Route-finding & map reading
  • Snow science & pit analysis
  • Skinning technique
  • First aid & self-rescue

Essential gear checklist

Backcountry skiing demands specialized equipment. Here is a tiered list of everything you need to get started safely:

  1. Safety essentials — never leave the trailhead without these
    • Avalanche transceiver (beacon) — worn on your body, not in your pack
    • Collapsible avalanche probe (at least 240 cm)
    • Avalanche shovel with metal blade
    • Avalanche airbag pack (optional but highly recommended)
  2. Touring setup
    • Touring skis — lighter than resort skis for easier uphill travel
    • Tech (pin) bindings or frame touring bindings
    • Climbing skins — mohair or nylon blend
    • Backcountry ski boots with walk mode
  3. Navigation & communication
    • Topo map and compass (and the skills to use them)
    • GPS device or loaded phone app (Gaia GPS, CalTopo)
    • Satellite communicator (Garmin inReach, SPOT)
  4. Clothing layers
    • Moisture-wicking base layer
    • Insulating mid-layer (down or synthetic)
    • Waterproof hard shell jacket and pants
    • Helmet, goggles, and gloves
  5. Pack contents
    • Water (2L minimum) and high-calorie snacks
    • First aid kit
    • Emergency bivy or space blanket
    • Headlamp with extra batteries
